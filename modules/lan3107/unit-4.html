Learning objectives

  • Describe the projects carried out by different departments in a company.
  • Give updates on the progress of a work project.
  • Describe project arrangements using appropriate tenses and language.
  • Use key vocabulary related to projects and company organisation.

Departments and their projects

Every department in a company carries out specific projects that support its core functions. Being able to describe what your department does — and what it is currently working on — is an important communication skill in any professional setting.

DepartmentTypical projects
Human Resources (HR)
  • Hiring and training new employees.
  • Setting up guidelines for handling staff complaints.
  • Reviewing staff benefits packages.
Information Technology (IT)
  • Updating the company website.
  • Upgrading internal systems and software.
  • Improving cybersecurity measures.
Product Development
  • Developing new products or services.
  • Conducting market research and product testing.
  • Improving existing product features.
Sales
  • Developing sales plans and strategies.
  • Expanding into new markets or client bases.
  • Reviewing and improving the sales process.
Marketing
  • Creating advertising campaigns.
  • Managing the company's social media presence.
  • Organising promotional events.

Describing a department's project

Use the following pattern to introduce a project your department is working on:

Template

'The [department] department is currently working on [project]. The aim of the project is to [goal]. It involves [key activities].'

Example

'The HR department is currently working on a new employee training programme. The aim of the project is to help new staff settle in more quickly. It involves developing an online induction course and a mentorship scheme.'

Giving project updates

In the workplace, you will often be asked to give a brief verbal update on how a project is progressing. A clear update covers three things: what has been done, what is happening now, and what comes next.

Structure of a project update

  1. What has been completed — use the present perfect tense.
  2. What is currently happening — use the present continuous tense.
  3. What will happen next — use will or going to for future plans.

Example update

'We have completed the initial market research and drafted the campaign brief. At the moment, we are finalising the creative concepts with the design team. Next week, we will present the proposals to the client for approval.'

Language for project updates

StageTenseUseful phrases
Completed work Present perfect 'We have finished …', 'We have already …', 'The team has completed …'
Current work Present continuous 'We are currently …', 'The team is working on …', 'We are in the process of …'
Planned work will / going to 'We will …', 'We are going to …', 'The next step is to …'

Reporting a challenge or delay

  • 'We have encountered a setback with …, but we are working to resolve it.'
  • 'The project is slightly behind schedule because …, however we expect to be back on track by …'
  • 'We are facing a challenge with …, so we may need to adjust the timeline.'

Describing project arrangements

Arrangements are plans that have already been decided or organised. Use the present continuous to describe firm future arrangements, and going to for intentions or plans that are less fixed.

Key rule — present continuous for arrangements

Use the present continuous to describe a future event that has already been arranged (a time, place, or participant has been confirmed).

StructureExample
Subject + be + verb-ing + time/place'We are meeting the client on Thursday afternoon.'
Subject + be going to + base verb'We are going to finalise the budget next week.'

Time expressions for project arrangements

  • '… by the end of this week / next Monday / the end of the month.'
  • '… in the next two weeks / in the coming days.'
  • '… on [day] / at [time] / before [deadline].'

Useful expressions

Asking about a project

  • 'Could you tell me more about what your department is working on at the moment?'
  • 'How is the [project name] project coming along?'
  • 'What stage are you at with [project]?'

Giving a project update

  • 'We have made good progress on …'
  • 'We are currently in the [planning / development / testing / final] stage.'
  • 'Everything is going according to plan.'
  • 'We are slightly behind schedule, but we expect to …'

Describing responsibilities within a project

  • 'I am responsible for …'
  • 'My role in this project is to …'
  • 'I am working closely with the [team/department] to …'

Useful vocabulary

Word / phraseMeaning
projectA planned piece of work with a specific goal and deadline.
milestoneAn important stage or point in the progress of a project.
deadlineThe time by which a task or project must be completed.
timelineA schedule showing when tasks will be completed.
progressForward movement towards a goal.
setbackA problem or delay that slows progress.
stakeholderA person or group with an interest in the outcome of a project.
briefA document outlining the goals, scope, and requirements of a project.
proposalA plan or suggestion put forward for consideration.
collaborationWorking together with others towards a shared goal.
objectiveA specific, measurable goal that a project aims to achieve.
scopeThe range of activities and outcomes that a project covers.

Useful verbs for describing project work: analyse, compile, coordinate, deliver, develop, draft, evaluate, implement, monitor, present, review, submit.
