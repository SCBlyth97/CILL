LAN3107

Essential Workplace Communication: Effective Oral Interaction

Offered in: Semester 1

What this module is about

This module develops the oral communication skills you need to function confidently in a professional workplace. Starting from a first day at work, it builds outward — greeting and introducing colleagues, describing job duties, making and receiving phone calls, participating in meetings, and handling workplace situations such as giving instructions, offering and asking for help, and making requests politely. Throughout, pronunciation strategies help you sound clear and natural when speaking English at work.

By the end of this module you will be able to:

  • Greet colleagues, introduce yourself and others, and end conversations politely.
  • Describe your job duties and the functions of different workplace departments.
  • Make, receive and take messages in phone calls.
  • Participate in workplace meetings — agreeing, disagreeing, asking for clarification and summarising.
  • Give and follow workplace instructions clearly and politely.
  • Offer help, make requests, and respond to both appropriately.
  • Apply pronunciation strategies including syllable and word stress, linking, and sentence stress.

The twelve units

Unit 1

First Day at Work

Greeting colleagues, introducing yourself, workplace vocabulary, departments, and syllable and word stress.

Notes Unit 2

Making Small Talk

Small talk strategies, active listening, question types, intonation, and conversation etiquette.

Notes Unit 3

Sharing about Culture

Cultural etiquette around the world, sharing Hong Kong culture with colleagues, and useful cultural vocabulary.

Notes Unit 4

Talking about Work Projects

Departments and projects, giving project updates, describing project arrangements, and key project vocabulary.

Notes Unit 5

Expressing Opinions

Stating and supporting opinions, verb-noun collocations, connected speech, and workplace policies and benefits.
